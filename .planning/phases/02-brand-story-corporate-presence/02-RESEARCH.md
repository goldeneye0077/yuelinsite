# Phase 2 Research

## What The Homepage Must Answer Fast

Within the first screen, the homepage should answer four questions:

1. What does the company do?
2. Who does it serve?
3. What kinds of products or services does it cover?
4. Where should the user go next if they want to talk?

## Brand Story Principles

- Brand name and business direction must outrank decorative visuals.
- The logo should appear as a real asset, not a symbolic placeholder.
- Cooperation brands should be presented as contextual trust signals, not a loose logo cloud.
- The address and company profile should feel factual and grounded, especially for industrial buyers who need supplier confidence.

## Layout Direction

- Hero should be full-bleed and poster-like.
- Support content should use line-based layout instead of heavy cards.
- Product directions should be visible on the homepage, but compact enough to avoid replacing the future product center.
- Final CTA should keep one clear consultation route instead of multiple competing forms.

## Risks To Avoid

- Generic SaaS hero patterns
- Overexplaining instead of showing structure
- Banner sections that repeat the same promise
- Partner logos without narrative value
