# Phase 3 Research

## Taxonomy Strategy

- The five top-level families follow the user's confirmed business IA, not the reference site's exact top-level menu.
- Inside the industrial-sensor family, subgroup names and child series should stay close to the reference page so later navigation and content entry stay grounded.
- Safety/area sensors and safety door products are split out into the dedicated safety-protection family.
- Laser-ranging and TOF-related products are split into the dedicated laser-ranging family.

## Reference Mapping Principles

- Keep reference-synced sensor groups factual and recognizable.
- Avoid pretending the reference source covers linear guides or pneumatic components when it does not.
- Mark linear-guide and pneumatic groups as first-pass inferred structures so they can be replaced by real catalog data later without reworking the data model.

## UI Direction For 03-01

- Do not jump straight into a finished product-center landing page.
- Use a taxonomy-preview section on the current product-center route to prove the data model works in UI.
- Keep the layout list-led and editorial rather than introducing product cards too early.

## Risks To Avoid

- Mixing safety and laser groups back into the industrial-sensor family in a way that conflicts with the chosen five-family IA
- Creating a data model that works only for one preview layout and cannot support later category pages
- Shipping a generic placeholder page after the taxonomy is already known
